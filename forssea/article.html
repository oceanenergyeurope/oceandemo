Forssea

    Test site: SEM-REV
    Current TRL: 6

    Success Story

    Forsseas ROVs have been qualified for autonomous surveys in shallow water in 2021 by several oil & gas offshore contractors. The next step was to trial and demonstrate Forsseas capacity to pre-program and remotely supervise an underwater visual acquisition campaign of an offshore floating wind site.

    Through Ocean DEMO, Forssea demonstrated its Argos ROV capacities in an industrial environment: Floatgen. Developed by BW Ideol and installed at SEM-REV since 2018, Floatgen was the only offshore wind turbine, whether fixed or floating, to be installed in France at that time.

    Thanks to its autonomous capacities, the ROV maintained a good stability and captured high quality images that allowed to visually confirm the integrity of submerged equipment. The Interreg Ocean DEMO project has also allowed cole Centrale de Nantes to partner with Forssea Robotics to deploy other smart technologies to service offshore wind farms.

    Next steps

    Forssea validated all the Argos ROVs hardware and software aspects in a real offshore wind environment and during a 4-day operation. They will capitalise on this success and enter commercial deployment.

    This also allows them to start a new R&D phase focused on remote control, advanced autonomous control (coming with a full mission management software suite) and battery powered vehicle.
