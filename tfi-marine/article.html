    Duration: 6 weeks

    Success Story

    TfI Marine installed a prototype power take off device (Gator) onto a mooring line on a TSB buoy in Scapa Flow, Orkney. Additionally a TfI Marine SeaSpring (D400) was installed on a mooring line optimised to receive the maximum potential load.

    For its 1st open-sea test, The Gator performed better than expected and the test demonstrated the soundness of the design. Further refinement will be necessary but the process from prototype to commercial product has been significantly advanced.

    The D400 SeaSpring is designed to reduce the stress on a mooring line, reducing loads and shock to the structures. The D400 spring saw loads in excess of its design load and continued to operate, validating the design and manufacturing of the polymer spring against rope.

    Next steps

    Tfi Marine will be able to improve the Gators design and look for potential partners in the offshore energy management area. There is a lot of potential in aquaculture, by displacing diesel generators and reducing the amount of diesel used, and in Floating Offshore Wind to power platform sensors independently of the turbine and assist turbine start up from Gator power storage systems.

    With the validation of the design and manufacturing process, TfI will be commercialising the D400 for the aquaculture sector and apply the learning from this scale to larger scale SeaSprings (D1200, D1800) for the Floating Offshore Wind market.
