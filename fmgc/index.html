    Success Story

    During the test period at SEM-REV, FMGC studied the stability of cast iron shells for subsea cables. Different section of protection cable and a control section have been installed to analyse the impact of the different section of weight.

    The results proved the great stability of the lines, even with difficult storm conditions. This stability is also reflected in how well cables were protected, especially regarding the minimum bending radius.

    On the non-embedded parts, the colonisation of the shells is relatively important, but does not disturb the stability or their life span. 

    Next steps

    By analysing the shells, FMGC has been able to refine their corrosion and abrasion models. This will allow them to ensure a long service life with optimal cable protection.
