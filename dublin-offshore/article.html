    Success Story

    Dublin Offshore, recognising the challenge facing Floating Offshore Wind (FOW) moorings, started an accelerated technology development plan to deliver the step change in Levelised Cost of Energy (LCOE) required to compete with fixed bottom offshore wind.

    The scope of the Ocean DEMO project was to design, supply and install the mooring system and floating platform, to integrate the LRD and to collect the data required to validate the LRD performance. Testing was carried out at the SmartBay test-site in the Atlantic Ocean off Irelandâ€™s west coast.

    The LRD delivered 1200 hours of passive operation without any degradation of performance including weathering Hurricane Epsilon which brought full-scale equivalent waves of 22 metres height. Independently validated operational data was recovered, both in real time and following recovery of the LRD, showing excellent agreement between the operation in the real world and the modelled behaviour.

    Next steps

    Ocean DEMO delivered the LRD to Technology Readiness Level 7 within a year of concept initiation, overleaping long established technology developers in the mooring component business.

    Dublin Offshore now looks forward, inviting partnerships and tender opportunities, with the confidence borne from delivering an ambitious programme with a new technology offshore in the face of unprecedented adversity, knowing they can deliver.

    Multi-device plans

    The LRD is ready for multi-device deployment. A major barrier to further testing of the LRD is access to a suitably sized floating wind platform. This will be the case for much of floating wind technology development. Support to access such platform would be a major step forward for the LRD development.
