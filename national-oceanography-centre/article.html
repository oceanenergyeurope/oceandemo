National Oceanography Centre

    Test site: SmartBay
    Duration: 1 month
    TRL progress: 4 > 5

    Success Story

    The objective of the National Oceanography Centres Ocean DEMO test programme was to demonstrate the capabilities of their optoelectronic interrogation system to record data using a legacy marine cable that could provide granular insights into a variety of parameters sustainably, e.g., cable health, marine noise, seabed temperature etc. The system was connected to an existing seafloor cable installed at the SmartBay test site.

    Their system comprises a unique combination of distributed optical fibre sensing features with bespoke machine learning algorithms to coherently capture a large data stream of information. This information was then compared with data collected from the existing sensors installed at SmartBay in order to validate the technology.

    Their capability introduces a truly disruptive ability to continuously, reliably and simultaneously monitor several natural and anthropogenic activities, and it will have far-reaching benefits for maritime industries.

    Next steps

    This trial opened new avenues for further research and commercial use cases, such as monitoring cable health for offshore renewable energy installations.

    The National Oceanography Centre is now looking to validate the system in different environments and secure more funding for continued analytics work.
