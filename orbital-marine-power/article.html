Orbital Marine Power

    Test site: EMEC
    Duration: 4 ½ years
    TRL progress: 6 > 7
    Leveraged funds: €16.000.000

    Success Story

    Orbital Marine Power installed the O2 2MW floating tidal turbine in January 2019 and has been continuously testing it since. The O2 features a significant reconfiguration over existing designs, in that the nacelles can be extended above the water surface for on-site access and are designed for low cost removal with workboat vessels.

    The test programme aims to demonstrate the benefits of the new design for ease of access to all subsystems, as well as developing associated operations procedures. Several innovative subsystems were tested, such as a new variable blade pitch control and a new mooring configuration.

    The O2 tidal technology is a low cost, low risk and commercially viable approach to generating tidal stream energy. It will require ongoing cost reduction, but the O2 provides a blueprint for unlocking competitive tidal stream generation.  

    Next steps

    The O2 tidal turbine will operate as a long term project of up to 15 years duration in Orkney. It will provide valuable evidence on long term performance and provide an opportunity to trial and de-risk offshore maintenance procedures. Overall, it will increase investor confidence in the sector.  

    Multi-device plans

    Orbital has secured contracts to install 3 more tidal devices at the EMEC test site in the next 2 to 3 years.
